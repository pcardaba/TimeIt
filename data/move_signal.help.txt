move_signal  -name signal_name
             -direction up|down
             [-help]


Moves a signal one position up or down in the diagram. The order of the signals is
otherwise implicit: it is the order in which the create_clock / create_input /
create_output commands appear in the script.

  signal_name : Mandatory. The name of the signal to move.
  direction   : Mandatory. Choice:
                  up   : Moves the signal one position towards the top.
                  down : Moves the signal one position towards the bottom.
                A signal that is already at the top (up) or at the bottom (down) of
                the diagram stays where it is: this is not an error.

A signal must always stay BELOW the clocks it refers to, so a move that would break
that rule is refused:
  - An input/output signal can not be moved above its launch or capture clock.
  - A clock can not be moved below a signal that it launches or captures, nor below
    a clock generated from it.
The reference clock may be hidden and still forbid the move.


Examples:


1.
   move_signal -name clk -direction down

Moves the clock "clk" one position down.


2.
   move_signal -name {data_out} -direction up
   move_signal -name {data_out} -direction up

Moves "data_out" two positions up.
